## Ingredients

- 1 ½  red onion, sliced
- 1 pound uncooked medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1 teaspoon ground turmeric, or to taste
- salt to taste
- ¼ cup water, or as needed
- olive oil for frying
- 1 tablespoon butter, or as needed
- 3  jalapeno peppers, thinly sliced
- 2 ½ tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
- 2 ½ teaspoons cayenne pepper
- 2 tablespoons ground coriander
- 1 tablespoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 5 tablespoons lime juice
- 1 tablespoon white vinegar
- 1 teaspoon soy sauce
- ½ teaspoon red food coloring
- 1  cinnamon stick
- 2 teaspoons poppy seeds
- 1 teaspoon coconut milk powder
- 2  cardamom pods
- 2  whole cloves
- 1  star anise pod
- ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
- ½ teaspoon dried oregano
- ½ teaspoon dried rosemary

## Instructions

1. Puree red onion in a blender or food processor.
2. Season shrimp with about 1/2 of the turmeric and salt.
3. Heat a skillet over medium heat.
4. Add shrimp, the remaining turmeric, and water.
5. Season with salt; simmer until water evaporates, 6 to 8 minutes.
6. Heat oil and butter in a separate skillet over medium heat.
7. Add pureed onions, jalapenos, ginger-garlic paste, cayenne pepper, coriander, cumin, and black pepper.
8. Cook and stir until golden brown, about 7 minutes.
9. Combine cinnamon stick, poppy seeds, coconut milk powder, cardamom, cloves, star anise, nutmeg, oregano, and rosemary in a separate saucepan over medium heat.
10. Cook, stirring frequently, until masala is aromatic, 5 to 7 minutes.
11. Pour masala into the onion-jalapeno mixture; cook, covered, over medium heat until flavors blend, about 7 minutes.
12. Add shrimp, lime juice, vinegar, soy sauce, and red food coloring; cook and stir until flavors are absorbed, about 5 minutes more.

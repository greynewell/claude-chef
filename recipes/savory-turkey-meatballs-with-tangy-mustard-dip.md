## Ingredients

- 1 slice day-old whole-grain bread, torn into 2-inch pieces
- ¼ cup 1% low-fat milk
- 1  onion, quartered
- 1  red bell pepper, seeded and quartered
- 2 cloves garlic
- 1 pound 93% lean ground turkey
- 1  egg, lightly beaten
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh Italian parsley
- 1 tablespoon grainy Dijon mustard
- ¾ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
- cooking spray
- ⅓ cup non-fat Greek yogurt
- ¼ cup light mayonnaise
- 4 teaspoons grainy Dijon mustard

## Instructions

1. Place torn bread in a food processor and pulse until fine crumbs form.
2. Transfer bread crumbs to a large bowl and add milk; set aside until bread is completely soaked.
3. Place onion, red bell pepper, and garlic in a food processor and pulse until finely chopped; stir into bread mixture.
4. Add turkey, egg, parsley, 1 tablespoon mustard, salt, and pepper to bread crumb mixture and mix well.
5. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour.
6. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C).
7. Spray a rimmed baking sheet with cooking spray.
8. Shape turkey mixture into 1-inch balls; arrange on the prepared baking sheet.
9. Bake in the preheated oven until firm and cooked through, about 15 minutes.
10. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
11. Let meatballs stand for 5 minutes.
12. Mix yogurt, mayonnaise, and 4 teaspoons mustard together in a bowl; serve alongside meatballs.

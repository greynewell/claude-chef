## Ingredients

- ½ cup water
- 2 teaspoons instant yeast
- 2 teaspoons white sugar
- 2 cups cake flour, divided
- ⅔ cup water
- 3 slices fresh ginger root, peeled and minced
- 1  spring onion, minced
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
- 1 tablespoon white sugar
- 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon corn flour
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 pinch ground white pepper to taste
- 7 ounces Chinese barbeque pork, cut into very thin slices
- ½ cup corn flour
- ⅜ cup cake flour
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- ⅜ cup white sugar
- 2 ½ tablespoons cooking oil

## Instructions

1. Combine 1/2 cup water, yeast, 2 teaspoons sugar, and 1 teaspoon cake flour in a large bowl; let stand until top is beginning to form a layer of white foam, about 20 minutes.
2. Add remaining cake flour (2 cups minus the 1 teaspoon) and stir gently using chopsticks until dough comes together.
3. Knead dough until smooth.
4. Cover with plastic wrap and let first dough stand in a warm place for 4 to 6 hours.
5. Mix 2/3 cup water, ginger, spring onion, oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon sugar, all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon corn flour, sesame oil, and white pepper in a saucepan over medium-low heat; cook, stirring constantly, until thickened, about 5 minutes.
6. Cool filling to room temperature; refrigerate.
7. Spoon chilled filling over pork; mix well.
8. Sift 1/2 cup corn flour, 3/8 cup cake flour, and baking powder together into a bowl.
9. Add 3/8 cup sugar and cooking oil and mix well.
10. Mix corn flour mixture into first dough, kneading until smooth.
11. Roll dough into a ball.
12. Cover ball with an inverted bowl and let stand for 10 minutes.
13. Divide dough into 12 to 15 pieces and roll each into a ball.
14. Flatten the balls into rounds.
15. Spoon 1 to 2 tablespoons filling into the center of each round and fold round around filling and seal.
16. It's fine if the top is a bit thick, it just helps create the top flower look.
17. Line bamboo steamers with parchment paper and arrange buns on top.
18. Set aside in a warm place for 5 minutes.
19. Close lids tightly.
20. Bring 1 1/2 to 2 quarts water to a boil in a wok or large skillet for 10 minutes.
21. Place steamers in the wok and steam buns over medium heat until cooked through, 12 to 15 minutes.

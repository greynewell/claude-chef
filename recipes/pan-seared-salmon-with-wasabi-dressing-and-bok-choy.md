## Ingredients

- ¼ cup mayonnaise
- 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
- 1 teaspoon wasabi paste
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- ½ teaspoon grated ginger
- 1 tablespoon water, or as needed
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 2  scallions, thinly sliced, whites and greens separated
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- ½ teaspoon grated ginger
- 2 heads   bok choy, ends trimmed and leaves separated
- 1 tablespoon water
- 4 (6 ounce) salmon fillets
- salt and ground black pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Combine mayonnaise, lime juice, wasabi paste, 1 clove garlic, and 1/2 teaspoon ginger in a small bowl.
2. Add just enough water to give dressing a drizzling consistency.
3. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium heat.
5. Add white parts of the scallions, 2 cloves garlic, and 1/2 teaspoon ginger.
6. Stir until softened, about 2 minutes.
7. Add bok choy and 1 tablespoon water.
8. Cook until leaves wilt, about 3 minutes.
9. Season with salt.
10. Transfer bok choy mixture to a plate.
11. Heat remaining 1 tablespoon oil in the same skillet over medium-high heat.
12. Pat salmon dry with paper towels; season with salt and pepper.
13. Place salmon skin-side down in the skillet.
14. Cook until skin is crispy, about 6 minutes.
15. Flip salmon and reduce heat to medium.
16. Cook until flesh is opaque, about 4 minutes more.
17. Transfer salmon to serving plates; drizzle wasabi dressing on top.
18. Garnish with green parts of the scallions.
19. Serve bok choy mixture alongside.

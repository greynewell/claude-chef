## Ingredients

- 1 (16 ounce) package dried flat rice noodles (such as ban pho)
- ½ cup fish sauce
- ¼ cup agave syrup
- 1  lime, juiced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon coconut oil
- 1  egg, beaten
- 2 tablespoons coconut oil
- 8 ounces raw chicken, thinly sliced
- 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
- 1  shallot, thinly sliced  (Optional)
- ¼ medium head cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1 cup shredded carrots
- 1 dash sriracha sauce, or to taste  (Optional)
- 2 tablespoons Thai basil
- ½ cup roughly chopped peanuts

## Instructions

1. Bring water to a boil in a large pot, about 5 minutes.
2. Remove from heat and toss in rice noodles; wait 5 minutes.
3. Stir and wait 5 minutes more.
4. Drain and rinse in a colander.
5. Combine fish sauce, agave syrup, lime juice, and garlic in a bowl and set aside.
6. Heat 1 tablespoon coconut oil in a small omelet pan over low heat.
7. Add egg and cook through without flipping, about 5 minutes.
8. Set aside on a cutting board until cooled, about 15 minutes.
9. Roll into a log and cut into thin strips.
10. Set egg aside.
11. Heat 2 tablespoons coconut oil in a skillet over high heat.
12. Add chicken, onion, and shallot and saute until fairly browned, 5 to 10 minutes.
13. Add cabbage, carrots, and drained noodles; saute until well combined, about 5 minutes more.
14. Add fish sauce mixture, egg slices, basil, and sriracha sauce to the saute pan with the noodle mixture.
15. Toss well, cover with a lid, and cook for 2 minutes.
16. Remove lid from saute pan and toss in peanuts.
17. Serve in the pan or transfer to a large platter.

## Ingredients

- 1 (2 pound) boneless pork loin roast
- ½ cup red salsa
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 2 tablespoons California chili powder
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 1 (6 ounce) can black olives, chopped
- 1 ½ cups sour cream, divided
- 1 ½ cups shredded Cheddar cheese, divided
- ½ cup green salsa
- 12  flour tortillas
- vegetable oil for frying
- 1 tablespoon guacamole, or to taste

## Instructions

1. Place pork roast into the bottom of a slow cooker.
2. Cover pork with red salsa, garlic, chili powder, and salt.
3. Cover the slow cooker and cook on Low until tender and cooked through, 4 to 6 hours.
4. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C).
5. Turn off slow cooker.
6. Remove any fat from pork and discard.
7. Transfer pork to a large bowl and shred well using 2 forks.
8. Let cool at least 10 minutes.
9. Pour olives, 1/2 cup sour cream, 1/2 cup Cheddar cheese, and green salsa over pork.
10. Mix well until thick and not runny.
11. Warm tortillas in a skillet over low heat to soften, 15 to 20 seconds per side.
12. Meanwhile, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat in a large frying pan.
13. Add a spoonful of pork mixture to each tortilla and roll up tightly.
14. Place rolled tortillas in hot oil and fry until lightly browned, 1 to 2 minutes per side.
15. Keep warm and serve with remaining 1 cup sour cream, 1 cup Cheddar cheese, and guacamole.

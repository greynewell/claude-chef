## Ingredients

- 1  potato
- 2 teaspoons vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon mustard seed
- ¼ teaspoon cumin seeds
- 2  carrots, finely diced
- 1  onion, finely chopped
- 1  green chile, finely chopped
- 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
- 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
- 1 teaspoon grated ginger
- ½ cup finely shredded cabbage
- ¼ (14 ounce) package firm tofu, crumbled
- ½ cup water
- ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- salt to taste
- 3 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 cup water
- 1 pinch salt

## Instructions

1. Place potato into a large pot and cover with salted water; bring to a boil.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until tender, about 20 minutes.
3. Drain and mash.
4. Set aside to cool.
5. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat.
6. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds and stir.
7. Add carrots, onion, green chile, garlic, turmeric, and ginger.
8. Cook and stir for 1 minute.
9. Add mashed potato, cabbage, and tofu and stir again.
10. Add water and cilantro; cook and stir until vegetables are soft and water is dried up, 7 to 8 minutes.
11. Add garam masala and salt and stir well.
12. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.
13. Mix flour, water, and salt together in a bowl.
14. Knead into a ball; continue kneading until dough is very soft, 10 to 12 minutes.
15. Wrap and rest for at least 1 hour.
16. Take a knob of dough and roll out into small round, about 4 inches in diameter.
17. Spoon a heaped teaspoon of filling in the center and moisten the edge with a little water.
18. Press edges together, overlapping the top slightly to form a pleat, and continue to create a half-moon shape with little pleats all around the edge.
19. Repeat with remaining dough and filling.
20. Fit a large pan with a steamer basket and add about 2 inches of water.
21. Bring to a boil.
22. Add dumplings and steam until swollen and cooked through, about 15 minutes.

## Ingredients

- 4 cups vegetable stock
- 1 teaspoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon curry powder
- 2 cups uncooked couscous
- 3 cups water
- 1 ½ cups uncooked quinoa
- 1 tablespoon salted butter
- 2 tablespoons slivered almonds, or to taste  (Optional)
- 5 stalks celery, finely chopped
- 1 ½ cups finely shredded carrots
- 1 cup drained garbanzo beans
- 1 cup chopped fresh parsley
- ½ cup raisins
- 3  green onions, finely sliced
- 1 tablespoon minced preserved lemon  (Optional)
- ⅔ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- ⅔ cup apple cider vinegar
- 6 tablespoons honey
- 2 tablespoons turmeric powder
- 2 tablespoons curry powder, or more to taste
- 1 tablespoon dry vegetable soup mix
- 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
- ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

## Instructions

1. Combine vegetable stock with 1 teaspoon olive oil and 1 teaspoon salt in a medium saucepan.
2. Bring to a boil; add 1 teaspoon turmeric and 1 teaspoon curry powder.
3. Stir in couscous and remove pan from heat.
4. Cover and let sit until water is absorbed completely, about 15 minutes.
5. Meanwhile, bring water to a boil in a pot and add quinoa.
6. Bring to a simmer, stirring occasionally.
7. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until water is absorbed, about 15 minutes.
8. Cover and let stand for a few minutes if there is liquid left in the pot.
9. Fluff couscous with a fork and place in a very large bowl.
10. Mix quinoa into the bowl; allow to cool.
11. Melt 1 tablespoon butter in a skillet over medium-high heat.
12. Saute almonds until toasted and golden brown, about 3 minutes.
13. Let cool and add to the couscous and quinoa.
14. Add celery, carrots, garbanzo beans, parsley, raisins, green onions, and preserved lemon.
15. Stir to combine.
16. Combine 2/3 cup olive oil, vinegar, honey, 2 tablespoons turmeric, 2 tablespoons curry powder, vegetable soup mix, 1 teaspoon salt, and pepper in a bowl.
17. Whisk together.
18. Toss dressing over the couscous mixture, a little at a time, stirring to combine.
19. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate to let the flavors develop, 8 hours to overnight.

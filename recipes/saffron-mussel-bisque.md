## Ingredients

- 2 pounds mussels, cleaned and debearded
- 1 ¼ cups white wine
- 1 ½ cups water
- 3 tablespoons margarine
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1  onion, chopped
- 1 clove garlic, crushed
- 1  leek, bulb only, chopped
- ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds, finely crushed
- 1 ½ tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 6  saffron threads
- 1 ¼ cups chicken broth
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
- salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons whipping cream

## Instructions

1. Place saffron threads in a small bowl, and cover with 1 tablespoon boiling water.
2. Set aside.
3. Scrub mussels clean in several changes of fresh water and pull off beards.
4. Discard any mussels that are cracked or do not close tightly when tapped.
5. Put mussels into a saucepan with wine and water.
6. Cover and cook over high heat, shaking pan frequently, 6-7 minutes or until shells open.
7. Remove mussels, discarding any which remain closed.
8. Strain liquid through a fine sieve and reserve.
9. Heat butter and oil in a saucepan.
10. Add onion, garlic, leek and fenugreek and cook gently 5 minutes.
11. Stir in flour and cook 1 minute.
12. Add saffron mixture, 2-1/2 cups of reserved cooking liquid and chicken broth.
13. Bring to a boil, cover and simmer gently for 15 minutes.
14. Meanwhile, keep 8 mussels in shells and remove remaining mussels from shells.
15. Add all mussels to soup  and stir in chopped parsley, salt, pepper and cream.
16. Heat through 2-3 minutes.
17. Garnish with parsley sprigs, if desired, and serve hot.

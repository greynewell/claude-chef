## Ingredients

- 7 ounces littleneck clams, in shell
- 2 (4 inch) pieces dashi kombu (dried kelp)
- 1 ⅓ cups water
- 1 (3 ounce) package enoki mushrooms
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 tablespoon chili powder
- 1 teaspoon minced garlic
- 1 (14 ounce) package tofu
- 5 small oyster mushrooms, stemmed, or to taste
- ½ teaspoon soy sauce
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- 1  spring onion, sliced on the diagonal
- 3 dashes ground black pepper
- 1 dash sesame oil

## Instructions

1. Place clams in a bowl with lightly salted water to cover.
2. Place kelp in a separate bowl with 1 1/3 cups water.
3. Let clams and kelp soak for 30 minutes.
4. Drain, reserving only the kelp water.
5. Cut off and discard the bottom 1 1/2 inches (roots) of the enoki mushrooms.
6. Wash the mushrooms.
7. Heat olive oil, chili powder, and garlic in a pot over medium heat.
8. Stir in the clams, kelp water, and tofu.
9. Bring to a boil.
10. Add enoki mushrooms, oyster mushrooms, soy sauce, and salt; bring to a boil.
11. Add spring onion, black pepper, and sesame oil.
12. Cook until flavors are intensified, 3 to 6 minutes more.

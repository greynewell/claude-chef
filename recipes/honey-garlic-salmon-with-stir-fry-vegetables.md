## Ingredients

- ½ cup honey
- 3 tablespoons reduced-sodium soy sauce, divided
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 3 teaspoons minced garlic, divided
- 1 ¼ pounds skin-on salmon fillet, uncut
- 1 tablespoon stir-fry sauce
- 1 (10 ounce) package frozen stir-fry vegetables
- salt and ground black pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C).
2. Place honey in a small microwave-safe bowl.
3. Microwave for 20 to 30 seconds.
4. Add 2 tablespoons soy sauce, lime juice, and 2 teaspoons garlic; whisk to combine.
5. Place salmon skin-side down on large piece of aluminum foil and fold sides up to hold sauce, keeping top of salmon uncovered.
6. Place the foiled salmon in a baking dish.
7. Spoon 1/2 the honey sauce on top.
8. Bake in the preheated oven for about 10 minutes.
9. While salmon bakes, heat stir-fry sauce in a medium skillet over medium-high heat.
10. Add remaining garlic; saute until fragrant, about 1 minute.
11. Add  ; toss to coat.
12. Reduce heat to medium; cook and stir vegetables for 1 more.
13. Add remaining soy sauce; season with salt and pepper.
14. Toss to coat.
15. Saute until vegetables are thoroughly heated through and moderately crunchy, 5 to 10 minutes.
16. Spoon some of the remaining honey sauce over salmon and bake for 5 minutes.
17. Adjust oven to broil and broil salmon until fish flakes easily with a fork, about 1 minute more.
18. Cut salmon into 4 pieces; plate and drizzle remaining sauce on top.
19. Serve with the stir-fry vegetables.

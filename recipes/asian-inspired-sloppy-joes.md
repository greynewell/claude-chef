## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 pound ground pork
- 3  scallions, trimmed and thinly sliced
- 1 (1 inch) piece minced fresh ginger root
- 1 stalk lemongrass, tough outer stalks removed, inner stalks thinly sliced
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- ¼ cup reduced-sodium soy sauce
- 3 tablespoons ketchup
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- 1 (10 ounce) package shredded cabbage
- 2 tablespoons wasabi-flavored mayonnaise (such as Trader Joe's®)
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 4  whole-wheat hamburger buns

## Instructions

1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
2. Add pork; cook and stir until pork is no longer pink, about 5 to 7 minutes.
3. Add scallions, ginger, lemongrass, and garlic powder; cook and stir until fragrant, 2 to 3 minutes.
4. Reduce heat to low.
5. Stir in soy sauce and ketchup.
6. Cook until flavors combine and mixture thickens slightly, 3 to 5 minutes.
7. Season with salt and pepper.
8. Combine cabbage, wasabi mayonnaise, rice vinegar, and lime juice in a large bowl; toss to mix slaw.
9. Split each bread roll and fill with pork mixture.
10. Top with slaw before serving.

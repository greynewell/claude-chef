## Ingredients

- 1 ¼ ounces dried shiitake mushrooms
- 1 cup cold water
- 3 ½ ounces dried soba noodles
- 3 cups water
- 1 stalk green onion, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 (3 inch) piece fresh ginger, sliced
- 2 tablespoons brown miso
- 1 tablespoon chile paste
- 1 ¾ cups chopped bok choy
- 1 small fresh red chile pepper, finely chopped
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil

## Instructions

1. Soak shiitake mushrooms in 1 cup cold water until soft, about 1 hour.
2. Drain mushrooms, reserving the soaking liquid.
3. Slice mushrooms into strips and set aside.
4. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
5. Add soba noodles and cook, stirring occasionally, until noodles are tender yet firm to the bite, 10 to 12 minutes.
6. While the noodles are cooking, combine 3 cups water, green onion, and ginger in a separate pot and bring to a boil.
7. Reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
8. Add shiitake slices, soaking liquid, miso, and chile paste; simmer for 2 to 3 minutes.
9. Add bok choy and simmer for 1 minute.
10. Brush 2 serving bowls with sesame oil.
11. Drain noodles and distribute into the bowls.
12. Top with shiitake slices, bok choy, and chopped red chile pepper.
13. Ladle hot broth over top.

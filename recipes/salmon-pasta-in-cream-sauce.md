## Ingredients

- 1 (8 ounce) package spaghetti
- 1 (8 ounce) salmon fillet, cut into cubes
- salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
- 3 tablespoons butter, divided
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- ½ small onion, diced
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- ¼ cup dry white wine, or to taste
- ½ cup heavy whipping cream
- 1 teaspoon tomato paste

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
2. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, 9 to 12 minutes.
3. Drain.
4. While spaghetti is cooking, season salmon with salt and pepper.
5. Heat 2 tablespoons butter and olive oil in a skillet over medium heat and cook salmon until opaque on all sides, about 5 minutes.
6. Transfer to a plate.
7. Melt remaining 1 tablespoon butter in the same skillet and cook onion until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
8. Add garlic and cook for 20 seconds.
9. Pour in wine and allow to evaporate.
10. Pour in heavy cream and cook for 2 minutes.
11. Stir in tomato paste and cook until sauce has desired consistency, 3 to 5 minutes.
12. Add salmon to the skillet and heat in the sauce.
13. Season salmon sauce with salt and pepper and serve with spaghetti.

## Ingredients

- ¼ cup miso paste
- ¼ cup canola oil
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar, or more to taste
- 1 tablespoon grated orange zest
- 1 tablespoon orange juice
- 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger root
- 1 teaspoon mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
- 1 teaspoon peanut butter
- 1 teaspoon chile garlic sauce
- 1 (8.8 ounce) package dried soba noodles
- 2 cups baby spinach
- 1 cup carrots, cut into matchstick-size pieces
- ½ cup shredded cabbage
- ½ cup sliced snow peas
- 8  cherry tomatoes
- 16  cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2  green onions, finely chopped
- 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds

## Instructions

1. Whisk miso paste, canola oil, rice vinegar, orange zest, orange juice, ginger, mirin, peanut butter, and chile garlic sauce together in a bowl until dressing is blended.
2. Bring water to a boil in a large pot.
3. Stir in soba noodles and return water to a boil.
4. Boil noodles until tender, 5 to 8 minutes.
5. Drain noodles in a colander under cold running water until cool, about 1 minute.
6. Toss cooked soba noodles with 1/2 of the miso dressing in a large bowl until thoroughly coated.
7. Divide spinach among 4 serving bowls; top each with 1/4 of the dressed noodles.
8. Divide carrots, cabbage, snow peas, and cherry tomatoes among each bowl; top each with 4 shrimp.
9. Drizzle remaining miso dressing onto each bowl to taste; sprinkle with green onions and toasted sesame seeds.

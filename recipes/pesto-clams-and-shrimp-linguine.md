## Ingredients

- 1 (16 ounce) box Barilla® Linguine
- 1 (6.3 ounce) jar Barilla® Traditional Basil Pesto
- 4 tablespoons butter, divided
- 1  small shallot, minced
- ¼ cup red bell pepper, minced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup white wine
- 1 (6.5 ounce) can chopped clams, drained with juice reserved
- ¾ cup chicken broth
- 1 (16 ounce) package clams in shell, scrubbed
- 1 pound uncooked medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
- ¼ cup chopped fresh basil leaves, plus more for garnish
- 2 tablespoons shaved Parmesan cheese

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
2. Cook linguine at a boil until tender yet firm to the bite, about 11 minutes; drain.
3. Heat 2 tablespoons butter in a skillet over medium heat.
4. Add shallot and bell pepper.
5. Cook until softened, 3 or 4 minutes; stir in garlic.
6. Add white wine and bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
7. Stir in canned clams, 1/2 of the reserved clam juice, and chicken broth.
8. Arrange fresh clams on the sauce.
9. Bring to a low simmer; reduce heat to low.
10. Cover and cook until clam shells open, 5 or 6 minutes.
11. Discard clams that do not open.
12. Stir in basil pesto and shrimp.
13. Heat through until shrimp are bright pink and opaque, 2 to 3 minutes.
14. Remove from heat and stir in remaining 2 tablespoons butter and chopped basil.
15. Distribute linguine among serving bowls; spoon clams, shrimp, and sauce over pasta.
16. Top with shaved Parmesan cheese and fresh basil.

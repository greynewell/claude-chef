## Ingredients

- ½ head lettuce, shredded
- 1 large tomato, chopped
- ½ large mango, chopped
- ½ large red onion, chopped
- ½  red bell pepper, chopped
- 1 cup chopped fresh cilantro
- 4 tablespoons mayonnaise, divided
- 2 tablespoons sriracha sauce, divided
- 1 teaspoon lemon juice, divided
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- ½ pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- ½ pound mahi mahi fillets
- 2 ½ tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, or to taste
- 1 tablespoon blackening seasoning (such as Louisiana Fish Fry Products)
- ½ teaspoon Cajun seasoning
- 2 (8 inch) flour tortillas, warmed

## Instructions

1. Combine lettuce, tomato, mango, red onion, red bell pepper, and cilantro in a medium bowl and mix thoroughly.
2. Add 2 tablespoons mayonnaise, 1 teaspoon sriracha sauce, and 3/4 teaspoon lemon juice; mix together.
3. Add salt and pepper.
4. Refrigerate salsa until ready to serve.
5. Mix 2 tablespoons mayonnaise, 1 tablespoon sriracha sauce, and remaining lemon juice in a small bowl.
6. Coat shrimp and mahi mahi fillets with 1 1/2 tablespoons olive oil and cover with blackening and Cajun seasoning.
7. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
8. Add fillets; cook until fish is golden, firm, and easily flaked with a fork, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
9. Place fillets on a plate.
10. Add shrimp to the pan; cook until opaque, about 3 minutes per side.
11. Spread a layer of the sriracha-mayonnaise sauce over each tortilla.
12. Add shrimp to 1 tortilla and mahi mahi to the other.
13. Add mango salsa to both.

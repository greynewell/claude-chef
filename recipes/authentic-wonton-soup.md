## Ingredients

- 1 (2 pound) pork tenderloin, or to taste
- ¼ cup hoisin sauce, divided
- 1 tablespoon red pepper flakes
- 1 pound lean ground pork
- 1 (4 ounce) can small shrimp, drained and chopped
- ¼ cup bread crumbs
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 2 tablespoons dried cilantro
- 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger
- 1 dash soy sauce
- 6 cups chicken stock
- 2 cups beef stock
- 1 (16 ounce) package wonton wrappers
- 1 cup chopped green onions

## Instructions

1. Rub pork tenderloin with 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce.
2. Season with red pepper flakes.
3. Place pork tenderloin in a slow cooker.
4. Cook on High until tender, 3 to 4 hours.
5. Cool until easily handled, about 10 minutes.
6. Chop into small pieces.
7. Mix remaining 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce, ground pork, shrimp, bread crumbs, rice vinegar, cilantro, ginger, and soy sauce together in a large bowl.
8. Place a 3/4-inch round ball of ground pork filling in the center of each wonton wrapper.
9. Brush edges with water; fold and seal.
10. Bring chicken stock and beef stock to a low boil in a large pot.
11. Stir in pork tenderloin.
12. Add wontons; cook until ground pork filling is no longer pink in the center, about 7 minutes.
13. Reduce heat to very low.
14. Serve soup garnished with green onions.

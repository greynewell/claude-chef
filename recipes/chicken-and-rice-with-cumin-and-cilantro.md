## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 20  chicken wings, tips removed and sections separated
- 2 ½ cups coarsely chopped onions
- ¼ cup coarsely chopped garlic
- ¼ cup chopped peeled ginger
- 2 cups long-grain white rice
- 1 ½ tablespoons ground cumin
- 2 ½ cups water
- 2 cups chopped cilantro stems, divided
- 1 (14 ounce) can diced tomatoes
- 1 tablespoon sriracha sauce
- 2 ½ teaspoons salt

## Instructions

1. Heat olive oil in a pot large enough to hold chicken wings in a single layer.
2. Add chicken wings; cook until browned, about 8 minutes.
3. Turn wings with tongs; cook until second side is browned, about 8 minutes more.
4. Transfer to a bowl using tongs.
5. Stir onions, garlic, and ginger into the drippings in the pot; cook until fragrant, 2 to 3 minutes.
6. Stir in rice and cumin.
7. Add water, 1 1/2 cup cilantro stems, tomatoes, sriracha sauce, and salt.
8. Return wings to the pot.
9. Bring broth to a boil.
10. Cover, reduce heat to low, and simmer until rice and wings are tender, about 30 minutes.
11. Garnish with remaining 1/2 cup cilantro stems.

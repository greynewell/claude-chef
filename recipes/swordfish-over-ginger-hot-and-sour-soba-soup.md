## Ingredients

- 4 cups water
- 2 (6 ounce) swordfish steaks
- salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons reduced-sodium vegetable bouillon
- 1 medium lime, juiced, divided
- 1 cup shredded carrots
- 1 cup bean sprouts
- 2 tablespoons reduced-sodium soy sauce
- 4  green onions, chopped
- 1  red jalapeno pepper, seeded and diced, or to taste
- 1 stalk lemongrass, split lengthwise
- 5 sprigs cilantro, stemmed and roughly chopped
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic, divided
- 1 ounce finely chopped ginger
- 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 5 ounces shiitake mushrooms, stemmed and sliced
- 2 heads   baby bok choy, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 14 ounces buckwheat noodles
- 3 drops sesame oil, or more to taste

## Instructions

1. Bring water to a boil in a pot.
2. Rinse swordfish and pat dry; season both sides with a pinch of salt and pepper.
3. Reduce boiling water to a simmer.
4. Stir in bouillon and 3/4 of the lime juice.
5. Add carrots, bean sprouts, soy sauce, green onions, jalapeno, lemongrass, cilantro, 2/3 of the garlic, and ginger.
6. Simmer broth for at least 10 minutes.
7. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat.
8. Add mushrooms; season with a pinch of salt and pepper and add remaining garlic.
9. Cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
10. Transfer mushrooms to a bowl.
11. Wipe the pan clean if there are bits stuck to the bottom.
12. Return pan to medium-high heat; add remaining olive oil and swordfish.
13. Cook until internal temperature reaches at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C), 4 to 5 minutes per side.
14. Squeeze remaining lime juice over the fish and set aside to rest.
15. Add mushrooms and bok choy to the broth.
16. Continue simmering on low, about 5 minutes.
17. Add soba noodles; cook until heated through, 5 to 7 minutes.
18. Add sesame oil.
19. Cut each block of noodles in half and divide between 4 bowls.
20. Ladle vegetables and broth over the top.
21. Cut swordfish steaks into equal pieces and place one on top of each bowl.

## Ingredients

- 1 pound uncooked medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon grated lime zest
- ½ (16 ounce) package dry rice stick noodles
- ¼ cup lime juice
- 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon grated lime zest
- ½ teaspoon anchovy paste
- ¼ teaspoon hot pepper sauce
- 1 ½ cups diced fresh peaches
- 1 cup shredded napa cabbage
- 2  carrots, shredded
- ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 16  cherry tomatoes
- 6  skewers

## Instructions

1. Preheat grill for medium heat and lightly oil the grate.
2. Combine shrimp, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, and lime zest in a bowl.
3. Allow shrimp to marinate at room temperature while preparing remaining ingredients.
4. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
5. Add rice stick noodles to boiling water and remove from heat.
6. Cook until noodles have cooked through, but are still firm to the bite, 3 to 5 minutes.
7. Rinse will cold water until chilled; drain well.
8. Whisk lime juice, remaining 3 tablespoons soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, remaining 1 teaspoon lime zest, anchovy paste, and hot pepper sauce in a large bowl.
9. Add peaches, cabbage, carrots, cilantro, basil, and garlic and toss to coat.
10. Stir rice noodles into vegetable mixture until combined; set aside.
11. Thread about 8 shrimp on 4 wooden skewer by piercing shrimp through the tail and the upper body.
12. Pierce cherry tomatoes onto remaining 2 skewers.
13. Place shrimp and tomato skewers on preheated grill.
14. Cook tomatoes until they begin to split, about 3 minutes per side; transfer to a platter.
15. Continue to cook shrimp until they are bright pink on the outside and is no longer transparent in the center, 2 to 3 minutes more.
16. Remove tomatoes and shrimp from skewers.
17. Transfer noodle salad to a large platter.
18. Surround salad with tomatoes and shrimp; drizzle any remaining lime dressing over shrimp.

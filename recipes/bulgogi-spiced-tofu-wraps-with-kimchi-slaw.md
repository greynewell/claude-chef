## Ingredients

- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 3 cups chopped leek, or to taste
- 3 tablespoons chopped onion, or to taste
- 2 tablespoons sesame oil
- 1 ½ tablespoons honey, or to taste
- ½ teaspoon black pepper
- 1 ½ (14 ounce) packages firm water-packed tofu, cut into 1-inch-thick slabs
- 1 ½ tablespoons honey, or to taste
- 1 ½ tablespoons light soy sauce
- 1 ½ tablespoons sesame oil
- 2 teaspoons gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)
- 1 (4 inch) cucumber, cut into matchsticks
- 2 small carrots, cut into matchsticks
- ⅓ cup matchstick-cut daikon radish
- 3  scallions, cut into matchsticks
- 12 leaves iceberg lettuce

## Instructions

1. Combine 3 tablespoons soy sauce, leek, onion, 2 tablespoons sesame oil, 1 1/2 tablespoons honey, and black pepper in a bowl.
2. Whisk until fully combined.
3. Add tofu and cover with plastic wrap.
4. Marinate in the refrigerator at least 2 hours.
5. Whisk 1 1/2 tablespoons honey, light soy sauce, 1 1/2 tablespoons sesame oil, and gochujang together in a bowl.
6. Toss in cucumber, carrots, daikon, and scallions.
7. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate slaw until ready to use.
8. Preheat an outdoor grill to 465 degrees F (240 degrees C) and lightly oil the grate.
9. Add the marinated tofu and grill until browned, about 4 minutes per side.
10. Let cool; chop into 1x3-inch strips.
11. Toss grilled tofu into the prepared slaw.
12. Wrap in lettuce leaves.

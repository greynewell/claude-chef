## Ingredients

- 2 small Meyer lemons, scrubbed
- 2 large  oranges
- 1 cup chopped fresh pineapple
- 1 cup fresh pineapple juice
- 1 cup fresh orange juice
- 12 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1  dried red chile pepper
- 2 cups dried penne pasta
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 medium leek, thinly sliced
- 1 cup cremini mushrooms, quartered
- ½ cup diced roasted red peppers
- 3 large cloves garlic, minced
- salt and ground black pepper to taste
- ¼ cup white wine
- 1 ½ cups heavy cream
- 2 cups packed arugula
- 1 ½ cups cooked salmon fillet, flaked
- ¼ cup sliced green onions
- 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese, or to taste

## Instructions

1. Zest and juice 1 lemon; zest remaining lemon and set aside.
2. Zest oranges, peel, then slice roughly.
3. Combine 1/2 of the orange zest and all the orange slices in small pot with chopped pineapple, pineapple and orange juices, lemon zest and juice from 1 lemon, fresh thyme, and dried pepper.
4. Bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer until juice is reduced by almost half.
5. Discard pepper.
6. Process remainder in a blender until smooth.
7. This is the citrus reduction base for your sauce.
8. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
9. Add penne and cook, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 11 minutes.
10. Drain.
11. Warm up oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
12. Add leek, mushrooms, red peppers, garlic, salt, and black pepper.
13. Saute until leeks and garlic are soft, about 5 minutes.
14. Deglaze with white wine.
15. Add remaining orange and lemon zest, 3/4 cup citrus reduction, and cream.
16. Bring to a boil, and cook until reduced to desired consistency.
17. Add cooked pasta, arugula, and salmon.
18. Toss until arugula wilts, and serve.
19. Top with green onions and Parmesan cheese.

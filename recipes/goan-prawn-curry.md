## Ingredients

- ½ pound medium shrimp, or more to taste
- salt to taste
- ¾ cup freshly grated coconut
- 4 small Kashmiri chile peppers, seeded and chopped
- 3 cloves garlic
- 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
- 5 peppercorns
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 small onion, sliced
- ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 ½ cups water
- 1 cup chopped okra
- 3 pieces kokum (wild mangosteen)

## Instructions

1. Peel, devein, and rinse prawns. Rub with a generous amount of salt.
2. Combine coconut, chile peppers, garlic, coriander seeds, and peppercorns in a food processor and process to a paste (masala).
3. Heat oil in a saucepan. Add onion and saute until browned, about 5 minutes. Add masala along with turmeric powder and stir-fry for 1 minute. Add water and bring to a boil.
4. Add shrimp and okra. Cover and simmer until prawns and okra are cooked, about 5 minutes. Add kokum and turn up the heat to bring to a boil again.
5. Turn off heat and let it stand for a few minutes until curry has thickened.

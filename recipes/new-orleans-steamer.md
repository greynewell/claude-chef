## Ingredients

- parchment paper
- cooking spray
- 2 cups cooked white rice
- 1 ½ teaspoons gumbo file powder
- 1 ½ teaspoons Cajun seasoning
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin
- 2  tomatoes, chopped
- 1  red bell pepper, sliced
- 1  green bell pepper, sliced
- 1  green onion, sliced
- 4  fresh okra, chopped  (Optional)
- 1 ½ teaspoons Cajun seasoning
- ½ pound crawfish, thawed if frozen
- ½ pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- ½ pound andouille sausage, sliced
- 1 ½ teaspoons Cajun seasoning
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin
- ¼ cup chicken broth
- 16  littleneck clams

## Instructions

1. Spray four 10x20-inch sheets of parchment paper with cooking spray.
2. Mix cooked rice, file powder, 1 1/2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning, 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder, and 1/2 teaspoon cumin in a bowl.
3. Divide rice mixture evenly over prepared parchment paper.
4. Toss tomatoes, red bell pepper, green bell pepper, green onion, and okra in a large bowl with 1 1/2 teaspoon Cajun seasoning until well coated.
5. Divide vegetable mixture evenly over rice.
6. Combine crawfish, shrimp, andouille sausage, remaining 1 1/2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning, 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder, and 1/2 teaspoon cumin in another bowl.
7. Divide seafood mixture evenly over vegetables.
8. Pour about 1 tablespoon chicken broth over seafood mixture and top each portion with 4 clams.
9. Fold parchment paper around seafood mixture, sealing edges to form a pouch.
10. Place the pouches on a microwave-safe platter.
11. Heat pouches in microwave on high until shrimp are bright pink on the outside and the meat is no longer transparent in the center, about 4 minutes.

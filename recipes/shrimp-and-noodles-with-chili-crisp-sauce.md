## Ingredients

- 1 (8 ounce) package spaghetti
- 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1 teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon baking soda
- 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon chili crisp oil sauce
- 1 teaspoon gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)
- 1 teaspoon white sugar
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 large shallot, finely chopped
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 (1 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and finely chopped
- 6  scallions, cut into 1-inch pieces

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, mix shrimp with salt and baking soda; let sit for 15 minutes.
3. Combine mayonnaise, soy sauce, chili crisp, gochujang, and sugar in a medium bowl. Set aside.
4. Drain spaghetti. Rinse shrimp with cool water and pat dry with paper towels.
5. Warm oil in a saute pan over medium heat. Cook and stir shallot, garlic, and ginger for 1 minute. Add shrimp and cook until bright pink and opaque, 4 to 5 minutes. Add scallions and cook for 1 minute longer.
6. Pour the chili crisp sauce over the shrimp. Mix in the cooked spaghetti and toss until evenly coated.

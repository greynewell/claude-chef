Engineer's Ramen

Prep 30min Cook 4hr 4 servings 520 cal

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg pork bones (neck or femur, split)
  • 200g chicken backs or wings
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 6 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 40g ginger, sliced
  • 60ml soy sauce
  • 30ml mirin
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 4 portions fresh ramen noodles
  • 4 soft-boiled eggs, halved
  • 200g chashu pork belly (see notes)
  • Nori sheets
  • Sliced scallions
  • Sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Blanch pork bones in boiling water for 10 minutes. Drain, rinse under cold water, and scrub off any scum. This step is critical for a clean broth.
  2. Place cleaned bones and chicken in a large stockpot. Cover with 3 liters of cold water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a vigorous simmer.
  3. After 1 hour, add onion, garlic, and ginger. Continue simmering for 3 more hours, maintaining the liquid level by adding water as needed. The broth should turn opaque and creamy.
  4. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve. Season with soy sauce, mirin, and sesame oil. Adjust to taste.
  5. Cook ramen noodles according to package directions. They should be firm, not soft.
  6. Divide broth among 4 bowls. Add noodles, then top with chashu, soft-boiled egg halves, nori, scallions, and sesame seeds.

Notes

For chashu: roll pork belly tightly, tie with butcher's twine, and braise in a mixture of soy sauce, sake, mirin, sugar, and water at 150C for 3 hours. Slice thinly when cool. For the eggs, boil for exactly 6 minutes 30 seconds, then ice bath immediately.

Gear

  • Large stockpot (at least 8-quart capacity)
  • Fine-mesh sieve or strainer
  • Chef's knife
  • Cutting board
  • Soft-boiled egg timer or pot
  • Ramen noodle bowls
  • Ladle

High-Protein Carbonara

Prep 10min Cook 20min 2 servings 680 cal

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti or rigatoni
  • 150g guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 whole eggs
  • 80g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
  • 30g Parmesan, finely grated
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a rolling boil. Cook pasta until 1 minute short of al dente. Reserve 200ml pasta water before draining.
  2. While pasta cooks, render the guanciale in a cold pan over medium heat. Cook until fat is translucent and edges are crisp, approximately 8 minutes. Remove from heat.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, whole eggs, Pecorino, and Parmesan until a thick paste forms. Add generous black pepper.
  4. Add drained pasta directly to the guanciale pan. Toss for 30 seconds off heat to coat in rendered fat.
  5. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta. Toss vigorously, adding pasta water one tablespoon at a time until a glossy, emulsified sauce coats each strand. The residual heat cooks the eggs without scrambling.
  6. Serve immediately with additional Pecorino and black pepper.

Notes

The key to carbonara is temperature control. If the pan is too hot, you get scrambled eggs. If too cold, you get a raw egg coating. Work off-heat and let the pasta's residual warmth do the work.

Gear

  • Large pot for pasta
  • Large skillet or sauté pan
  • Mixing bowl
  • Whisk
  • Pasta tongs or fork
  • Microplane or box grater
  • Measuring spoons and scale
